Help center

Getting started

REDCUT runs entirely in your browser tab. There is nothing to install and nothing gets uploaded. Chrome or Edge give you the widest format support; Safari works but records exports as MP4 with fewer options; Firefox exports WebM.

  • Open the editor and drop a video file anywhere on the page.
  • Drag it from the media panel onto track V1.
  • Press Space to play, Ctrl+K to split.
  • Press Ctrl+M to export.

Importing media

Supported formats are whatever your browser can decode. In practice that means MP4 and MOV with H.264, WebM, MP3, WAV, M4A, AAC, PNG, JPG and WebP. HEVC, ProRes and RAW formats generally will not decode in a browser; convert them first.

Imported files are cached in this browser's storage so projects reopen with media attached. Clearing site data clears the cache and your clips will ask to be relinked.

Editing

  • Move a clip by dragging it. Drag vertically to change tracks.
  • Trim by dragging either end of a clip.
  • Split at the playhead with Ctrl+K or Ctrl+B.
  • Ripple delete with Shift+Delete, which closes the gap.
  • Snapping toggles with S and catches clip edges, the playhead and markers.
  • Markers go down with M for beats you want to cut to.
V2 sits above V1, so put titles and overlays on V2. A1 and A2 are audio only. Mute and solo per track live in the track headers.

Audio and titles

Every clip has its own volume, speed, fade in and fade out in the inspector. The Audio tab generates royalty free effects on the spot: whooshes, risers, impacts, a lo-fi beat and an ambient pad. They are made with the Web Audio API and land in your media panel as WAV files.

The Titles tab adds a title card with a headline, subtitle, size and colour, and flat colour cards for intros and outros.

Exporting

Pick a resolution up to 4K, a frame rate and a bitrate. Export plays the timeline through once and records it, so a three minute cut takes three minutes. Keep the tab in the foreground; browsers throttle background tabs and you will get dropped frames.

File type depends on the browser. Chrome and Safari usually produce MP4; Firefox produces WebM, which every modern player and every social platform accepts.

Keyboard shortcuts

Premiere Pro and CapCut habits both work. Where the two conflict, both keys are bound.

Projects and saving

The editor saves continuously: on a timer, after every edit, when the tab is hidden and when you close it. A version is kept each time you export or save manually, and you can restore any of the last fifteen from the project menu on your dashboard.

Save file downloads a .redcut file containing your edit decisions. It does not contain the video itself, the same way a Premiere project file does not. Keep your source footage where it is and the project reopens fine.

Troubleshooting

  • Clip shows a warning triangle. Its source file is gone from the cache. Re-import the file with the same name.
  • Export is choppy. Close other tabs, keep this one focused, and drop the bitrate a step.
  • No audio in the export. Check the track is not muted and the clip volume is above zero, then export again.
  • A video imports with no picture. The codec is not supported. Convert to H.264 MP4.
  • Playback stutters on 4K source. Browsers decode 4K slowly. Proxy editing is on the roadmap.

FAQ

  • Does anything upload? No. Everything happens in your browser.
  • Is there a watermark? No, on any plan.
  • Does it work offline? Yes, once the page has loaded.
  • Can two people edit one project? Not yet, that needs a sync backend.
  • Phone or tablet? The site works, the editor asks for a desktop.
